Basic Map Navigation

Laptop/Desktop

  • While hovering over the map, use your mouse or trackpad to zoom in and out of the map.

Phone/tablet

  • Use two fingers in a pinch action on the screen to zoom in and out.

In addition to using the zoom options above, the map has + and - buttons that can be clicked to zoom in and out.

The layer button directly below the + and - buttons can be used to filter map layers to make it less cluttered if certain map layers are not required.

It is highly recommended to use the fullscreen option when using the application.

The Reset Map button can be used at anytime during map navigation to re-zoom to the initial starting point. This button will also clear and reset any changes/pop-ups that appear during the use of the app.

Map Feature - Communities

The main content of this app is contained in the community layer. Communities are represented on the map as grey circles with a large, transparent circular border. You can zoom in to gain a better view of communities that are clustered together, and evaluate the approximate location of the community headquarters.

The location of each community has been estimated. The exact point on the map often corresponds to something like the band office or a community hall. The large transparent circular border around the grey circle is used to indicate that the community is not neccecarily a pinpoint on the map.

When hovering over a community circle, a tooltip will appear with some preliminary information. The community name is listed at the top in bold, and if available, the phonetic information will apear in brackets next to the name indicating the proper way to sound out the community name.

When hovering over a community, the tooltip gives an indication that you can click directly on the circle to view additional information about the community. See additional instructions below.
